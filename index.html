Why AeroMind

New aircraft break the systems aviation depends on.

Route planning, insurance, financing, and dispatch all rest on one assumption: accumulated operating evidence. Electric aviation has none yet. Corridors are unplannable without route history. Individual aircraft are opaque without failure data. Every decision that keeps a fleet flying — underwrite, finance, dispatch, site infrastructure — structurally breaks.

AeroMind closes that gap with physics. Where data-driven methods have no signal to work with, a physics-grounded model of the aircraft and its environment produces calibrated, decision-grade intelligence from day one — and sharpens continuously as real operating data arrives.

The platform

One platform. Two intelligence layers.

A single physics-grounded core serves both the network-level questions — where can this aircraft profitably fly — and the per-aircraft questions — what is the condition of this specific tail, right now. Every neural-network output is accompanied by a certifiable provenance proof — the audit trail aviation regulation expects for AI-supported decisions.

Regulatory readiness

Certifiable provenance proofs — not black-box AI.

Aviation authorities do not accept unexplained model outputs. AeroMind generates a structured provenance record for each decision produced by the neural network — inputs, model version, physics constraints, uncertainty, and the reasoning path — so operators, lessors, insurers, and regulators can reconstruct why a corridor score or dispatch recommendation was made. Built for the assurance bar of EASA, FAA, and the broader AI-in-aviation conversation — not as an afterthought.

Technology

Physics first. Every decision, provable.

Conventional aviation intelligence calibrates on decades of accumulated failure data. AeroMind inverts the approach: start from the physics of the aircraft — the electrochemistry, the thermal behaviour, the aerodynamics — treat operating data as calibration, not prerequisite, and attach a certifiable provenance proof to every neural-network decision so the output remains auditable under aviation regulation.

01

Model

Multi-domain physics models of the electric powertrain and its operating environment, calibrated against aerospace-grade reference behaviour and built for the specific failure modes of electric aviation.

02

Calibrate

Every flight tightens the model. Telemetry and operational data continuously refine per-aircraft predictions — with quantified uncertainty at every step, so downstream decisions know exactly how much to trust each number.

03

Decide

Decision-grade outputs, shaped for the stakeholder: corridor economics for planners, state-of-health for operators, calibrated risk evidence for insurers and lessors. One core, many decisions.

04

Prove

For each neural-network decision, the platform emits a certifiable provenance proof — model identity, inputs, constraints, intermediate evidence, and confidence — suitable for operational audit, underwriting files, and regulatory review. No opaque recommendation without a trail.
